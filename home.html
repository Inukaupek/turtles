TURTLES

INTRODUCTION

Sri Lanka, an island nation located in South Asia, is home to several species of turtles.The sea turtles in Sri Lanka are an important part of the country's natural heritage, and efforts have been made to protect their nesting grounds and conserve their populations. The coastal areas of Sri Lanka, particularly in the southern and eastern regions, are popular nesting sites for sea turtles. In addition to sea turtles, Sri Lanka is also home to several species of freshwater turtles, including the Indian flapshell turtle, the Asian leaf turtle, and the common pond turtle. These turtles are often found in rivers, streams, and ponds across the island and play an important role in maintaining the health of their freshwater habitats. Overall, turtles in Sri Lanka are an important part of the country's rich biodiversity, and efforts are being made to protect them and their habitats for future generations.
