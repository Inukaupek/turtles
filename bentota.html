Bentota turtle hatchery

About Haatchery

The Bentota Turtle Hatchery is a renowned conservation center located in the coastal town of Bentota in Sri Lanka. It serves as a crucial sanctuary for sea turtles and plays a vital role in their protection, preservation, and education. The hatchery aims to combat the decline of sea turtle populations caused by human activities, such as habitat destruction, pollution, and poaching. It provides a safe haven for injured, disabled, and vulnerable sea turtles, nurturing them back to health and releasing them into the ocean when they are ready. Visitors to the Bentota Turtle Hatchery have the opportunity to witness various species of sea turtles up close, including the Green Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, Olive Ridley Turtle, Loggerhead Turtle, and Leatherback Turtle. The dedicated staff and volunteers at the hatchery provide valuable information about these magnificent creatures, their life cycles, and the challenges they face.

Things we can do

See Turtle Hatchlings

If you're lucky, you may witness the hatching of baby sea turtles. Watch as these tiny hatchlings make their way to the sea for the first time. The hatchery ensures a safe environment for the newborns, increasing their chances of survival.
