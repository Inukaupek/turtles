Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

About Haatchery

The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is a well-known turtle conservation center located in the village of Kosgoda in Sri Lanka. It serves as a significant sanctuary and rehabilitation facility for sea turtles and plays a vital role in their conservation and protection. The hatchery focuses on several species of sea turtles, including the Green Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, Olive Ridley Turtle, Loggerhead Turtle, and Leatherback Turtle. It is dedicated to combating the decline of sea turtle populations caused by human activities, such as habitat destruction, pollution, and poaching. Visitors to the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery have the opportunity to learn about the different species of sea turtles and their conservation needs through guided tours. Knowledgeable staff members provide valuable information about the life cycles of sea turtles, their nesting habits, and the threats they face in the wild.

Things we can do

Guided Tour

Take a guided tour of the hatchery, where knowledgeable staff will provide information about the different species of sea turtles, their life cycles, and the conservation efforts carried out by the hatchery. Learn about the challenges faced by sea turtles and the importance of their protection.

Learn about Nesting Practices

Gain insights into the nesting practices employed by the hatchery. Understand how the staff identifies and relocates sea turtle nests from vulnerable areas to the hatchery's protected nesting zone. Learn about the measures taken to safeguard the eggs and enhance their chances of successful hatching.

Witness Sea Turtle Conservation

Get an opportunity to see the efforts taken to protect sea turtles. Explore the different sections of the hatchery, including the nesting area and tanks that house injured, disabled, or rescued sea turtles undergoing rehabilitation.
